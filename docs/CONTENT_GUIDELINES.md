# Content Guidelines & Governance

## Foundational Principles

### Gurbani First
Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji is the supreme spiritual authority. Any historical narrative that contradicts Gurbani principles is suspect. All history must be interpreted through the lens of Gurbani.

> ⚠️ **Critical Principle**: If someone builds Sikh history ignoring Gurbani — they're already off track.

### We Are Curators, Not Authorities
This platform presents Sikh history and Gurbani interpretations from recognized sources. We do not create new interpretations or claim authority over religious matters.

### Source-First Always
Every piece of content must answer: "Says who?"

**For complete source hierarchy, see [SOURCES.md](./SOURCES.md)**

## Content Categories

### 1. Gurbani Content (Highest Sensitivity)

#### What We Include
- **Gurmukhi Text**: Unicode-encoded original text from Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji
- **Transliteration**: Roman script phonetic representation
- **Interpretations**: From named, recognized Teekas only

#### What We Do NOT Do
- Create new interpretations
- Accept user-submitted meanings
- Use AI-generated meanings without scholarly verification
- Call English text "translation" (use "meaning/interpretation")
- Allow comments, likes, or engagement metrics on Gurbani

#### Approved Interpretation Sources (Hierarchy)
1. **Prof. Sahib Singh Teeka** - Sri Guru Granth Sahib Darpan (1962)
   - Most respected grammatical Gurbani framework
   - Reduces mythological interpolation
   - Essential for disciplined interpretation
2. **Faridkot Teeka** (1905)
   - Traditional, Sanatan-influenced
   - Must be labeled clearly as interpretation
3. **Dr. Manmohan Singh** - English interpretation (SGPC)
   - Widely used English meanings
   - Interpretive, not literal
4. **Bhai Vir Singh** - Select works
5. **Sant Singh Maskeen** - Recorded and written katha
   - Philosophical and ethical depth
   - Not literal translation — vichaar source

#### Language Rules
| Language | Purpose | Label |
|----------|---------|-------|
| Punjabi (Gurmukhi) | Authoritative | "ਗੁਰਬਾਣੀ" |
| English | Interpretive | "Meaning based on [Source]" |
| Hindi | Secondary access | "अर्थ" |

**Never say**: "English translation of Gurbani"
**Always say**: "English meaning/interpretation based on [Teeka Name]"

### 2. Historical Content

#### Source Hierarchy for History

**Tier I - Absolute Primary (Non-negotiable)**
- Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji (spiritual lens for all history)

**Tier II - Classical Sikh Historical Granths**
- Sri Gur Panth Prakash – Rattan Singh Bhangu (1841)
- Suraj Prakash – Bhai Santokh Singh (1843)
- Bansavalinama – Kesar Singh Chhibber (1769)

**Tier III - Early Non-Sikh & Contemporary Accounts (Corroborative Only)**
- Dabistan-i-Mazahib (c. 1655)
- Persian Court Chronicles

**Tier IV - Colonial & Modern Academic (Use with Caution)**
- The Sikh Religion – Max Arthur Macauliffe (1909)
- A History of the Sikhs – Khushwant Singh (1963)

**Tier V - Modern Institutional**
- SGPC Publications
- Punjabi University Patiala
- Sikh Reference Library

#### Structure Requirements
All historical content follows:
```
Era
 └─ Period
     └─ Event
         ├─ Description (neutral tone)
         ├─ Key figures
         ├─ Date (with precision indicator)
         ├─ Primary sources (required)
         ├─ Secondary sources (optional)
         └─ Conflicting interpretations (if any)
```

#### Date Precision
Every date must indicate its precision:
- **Exact**: Day/Month/Year confirmed by sources
- **Year**: Only year is known
- **Approximate**: Estimated (prefix with "c." or "ਲਗਭਗ")
- **Disputed**: Different sources give different dates

#### Neutral Tone
Historical events are described neutrally:
- ❌ "The heroic battle of..."
- ✅ "The battle of... occurred on..."

- ❌ "The evil forces..."
- ✅ "The opposing forces..."

#### Conflicting Interpretations
When sources disagree:
1. Present each interpretation separately
2. Cite the source for each
3. Note the perspective/potential bias
4. Never merge into a single narrative

Example:
```
Interpretation 1 (Rattan Singh Bhangu, 1841):
"[Quote or summary]"
- Perspective note: Written by descendant of eyewitnesses

Interpretation 2 (Khushwant Singh, 1963):
"[Quote or summary]"
- Perspective note: Academic analysis, not primary source
```

### 3. Contemporary History (POST-1947)

Events from approximately 1947-present require special handling.

#### Mandatory Rules
1. **Multiple Sources Required**: No single-source claims
2. **Separation of Facts and Interpretations**: Facts first, interpretations labeled separately
3. **No Opinion Journalism**: Editorial pieces are not acceptable sources
4. **Traceable Documentation Only**: Everything must be verifiable

#### Preferred Source Types
- Court documents (official records)
- Public speeches (verbatim transcripts)
- Official statements (government, organizations)
- Chronological reporting (dated news from multiple outlets)
- Video/audio recordings (with clear provenance)

#### Editorial Standards
- **No glorification** of any figure or movement
- **No vilification** of any figure or movement
- **Only traceable facts** with clear attribution

#### Required Disclaimers
1. Must include: "This is evolving history, not final"
2. Must be reviewed more frequently (quarterly)

#### Special Handling Required
- Events involving living persons
- Ongoing movements and developments
- Recent (past 5 years) occurrences
- Politically sensitive events

## Source Citation Standards

### Required Fields
```
- Source Title (Gurmukhi + English)
- Author (if applicable)
- Source Type (Primary/Secondary/Teeka/Contemporary)
- Year (composed/published)
- Specific location (chapter/page/verse)
```

### Source Reliability Notes
Each source can have:
- **Reliability notes**: Scholarly assessment
- **Caveats**: Known biases or limitations

Example:
```
Source: "The Sikh Religion" by Max Arthur Macauliffe (1909)
Type: Secondary
Reliability: Scholarly work with direct access to Sikh sources
Caveat: Written from colonial-era perspective. Cross-reference with primary sources.
```

## Editorial Process

### New Content Workflow
```
Draft → Review → Verification → Publication
```

1. **Draft**: Content created with all required citations
2. **Review**: Editorial check for tone, completeness, attribution
3. **Verification**: Scholarly review for accuracy
4. **Publication**: Content goes live with full attribution

### Review Requirements by Content Type

| Content Type | Minimum Sources | Scholarly Review |
|--------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Gurbani interpretation | 1 named Teeka | Required |
| Historical event | 1 primary source | Required |
| Historical figure bio | 2 sources | Required |
| Contemporary event | 2 verifiable sources | Required + Ongoing |

### Content Status Definitions

- **DRAFT**: Being prepared, not visible to public
- **SCHOLARLY_REVIEW**: Awaiting expert verification
- **PUBLISHED**: Verified and publicly visible
- **REQUIRES_UPDATE**: Flagged for revision based on new information
- **DISPUTED**: Multiple conflicting sources, all shown

## Prohibited Content

### Absolutely Not Allowed
1. User-submitted Gurbani interpretations
2. AI-generated meanings without human verification
3. Anonymous historical claims
4. Editorialized or sensationalized narratives
5. Content that contradicts Sikh Rehat Maryada
6. Merged/synthesized interpretations that hide sources
7. Ads within Gurbani sections

### Requiring Special Approval
1. Content about living individuals
2. Politically sensitive contemporary events
3. Content disputing established sources

---

## Language Integrity

### Multilingual Content Rules

1. **Punjabi (Gurmukhi) is always required** for any multilingual content
2. Other languages supplement, never replace
3. Language switch must not alter meaning hierarchy
4. Romanization is phonetic aid only

### Translation vs Interpretation

| Term | Usage |
|------|-------|
| "Translation" | ❌ Never use for Gurbani English text |
| "Interpretation" | ✅ For Gurbani meanings |
| "Meaning based on [Source]" | ✅ Preferred phrasing |
| "Arth" (ਅਰਥ) | ✅ Punjabi term for meaning |

## Scholarly Review Request

Content may be flagged for review when:
1. Source reliability is questioned
2. New contradicting evidence emerges
3. Community feedback indicates error
4. Scheduled review period arrives

Process:
1. Content status changed to SCHOLARLY_REVIEW
2. Assigned to qualified reviewer
3. Review notes documented
4. Status updated accordingly
5. Audit log maintained

If at any point:
- Speed is prioritized over accuracy
- Reach is prioritized over roots
- Ambiguity is hidden instead of labeled

→ **Stop and request human scholarly review**
