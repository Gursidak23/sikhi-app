# Authoritative Sources for Sikh History & Gurbani

## Source Hierarchy

This document establishes the hierarchy of sources used by the Sikhi Vidhya Platform. Sources are organized by category and authority level. **Any historical narrative that contradicts Gurbani principles is suspect.**

---

## I. ABSOLUTE PRIMARY & FOUNDATIONAL SOURCES

*Non-negotiable. Everything else is secondary to these.*

### 1. Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji
- **Authority Level**: SUPREME
- **Type**: Primary Spiritual Source
- **Usage**:
  - Supreme spiritual authority in Sikh tradition
  - Primary source for Sikh philosophy, ethics, and worldview
  - Not a historical chronicle, but the lens through which all history must be interpreted
  - Any historical narrative that contradicts Gurbani principles is suspect
- **Status**: ਸਤਿਗੁਰੂ (Eternal Guru)

> ⚠️ **Critical Principle**: If someone builds Sikh history ignoring Gurbani — they're already off track.

---

## II. CLASSICAL SIKH HISTORICAL GRANTHS

*Written by Sikhs, for the Panth, close to the events.*

### 2. Sri Gur Panth Prakash – Rattan Singh Bhangu (1841)
- **Authority Level**: Primary Historical Source
- **Type**: Classical Sikh Granth
- **Author**: ਭਾਈ ਰਤਨ ਸਿੰਘ ਭੰਗੂ (Bhai Rattan Singh Bhangu)
- **Usage**: Essential for:
  - Misl period history
  - Khalsa ideology and development
  - Sikh-Afghan conflicts
  - 18th century Sikh consciousness
- **Perspective Note**: Written with Panthic perspective — acknowledged, not hidden
- **Reliability**: Written by descendant of eyewitnesses; backbone of Sikh historical consciousness

### 3. Suraj Prakash (Gur Pratap Suraj Granth) – Bhai Santokh Singh (1843)
- **Authority Level**: Primary Historical Source
- **Type**: Classical Sikh Granth
- **Author**: ਭਾਈ ਸੰਤੋਖ ਸਿੰਘ (Bhai Santokh Singh)
- **Usage**: Detailed narratives of Guru Sahiban
- **Caveat**: Rich but poetic — not literal history. Must be cross-referenced, not blindly quoted
- **Reliability**: Comprehensive historical account in verse; major source for Guru period history

### 4. Bansavalinama – Kesar Singh Chhibber
- **Authority Level**: Primary Historical Source
- **Type**: Classical Sikh Granth
- **Author**: ਕੇਸਰ ਸਿੰਘ ਛਿੱਬੜ (Kesar Singh Chhibber)
- **Usage**:
  - Genealogies of Guru Sahiban
  - Early Sikh tradition and practices
  - Early Panth structure
- **Reliability**: Valuable for early Panth documentation

---

## III. EARLY NON-SIKH & CONTEMPORARY ACCOUNTS

*Valuable for corroboration, not for authority.*

### 5. Dabistan-i-Mazahib (School of Religions)
- **Authority Level**: Corroborative Source
- **Type**: External Historical Account
- **Author**: Attributed to Mobad Shah or Mir Zu'l-Fiqar Ardistani
- **Year**: c. 1645-1658
- **Usage**:
  - External observation of early Sikhs
  - How Sikhs were perceived by contemporaries
  - Documentation of early Sikh practices
- **Caveat**: Not spiritually authoritative; external perspective only

### 6. Persian Court Chronicles
- **Authority Level**: Corroborative Source
- **Type**: Administrative/Political Records
- **Examples**:
  - Ahmad Shah Abdali's court records
  - Mughal administrative texts (Ain-i-Akbari, Akbarnama)
  - Tarikh-i-Punjab
- **Usage**:
  - Cross-checking dates
  - Verifying battles and political events
  - Understanding political context
- **Caveat**: Never used to interpret Sikh motives or theology

---

## IV. COLONIAL & EARLY MODERN HISTORIANS

*Useful, but potentially problematic if taken uncritically.*

### 7. The Sikh Religion – Max Arthur Macauliffe (1909)
- **Authority Level**: Secondary Source
- **Type**: Colonial-era Scholarship
- **Author**: Max Arthur Macauliffe
- **Usage**:
  - One of the more respectful colonial works
  - Preserved many translations and narratives
  - Useful for introductory understanding
- **Caveat**: Still filtered through Christian/colonial worldview. Cross-reference with primary sources required.

### 8. A History of the Sikhs – Khushwant Singh (1963-1966)
- **Authority Level**: Secondary Source
- **Type**: Academic Historical Work
- **Author**: Khushwant Singh
- **Usage**:
  - Strong on chronology and political history
  - Academic perspective on Sikh history
- **Caveat**: Weak on spiritual depth. Must never be treated as final authority.

> **Important**: Colonial and post-colonial academic works are contextual aids, not foundations.

---

## V. GURBANI INTERPRETATION & ARTH SOURCES

*Critical for Prakash & meaning features.*

### 9. Faridkot Teeka (1905)
- **Authority Level**: Authorized Teeka
- **Type**: Classical Commentary
- **Commissioned by**: Maharaja of Faridkot
- **Usage**:
  - Traditional, Sanatan-influenced interpretation
  - Classical understanding of Gurbani
- **Caveat**: Must be labeled clearly as "Faridkot Teeka interpretation"
- **Reliability**: Authoritative traditional interpretation

### 10. Sri Guru Granth Sahib Darpan – Prof. Sahib Singh (1962)
- **Authority Level**: Primary Teeka (Most Recommended)
- **Type**: Grammatical/Scholarly Commentary
- **Author**: ਪ੍ਰੋ. ਸਾਹਿਬ ਸਿੰਘ (Prof. Sahib Singh)
- **Usage**:
  - Most respected grammatical Gurbani framework
  - Reduces mythological interpolation
  - Essential for disciplined interpretation
- **Reliability**: Scholarly Teeka based on Gurmukhi grammar principles

### 11. Sant Singh Maskeen Katha
- **Authority Level**: Vichaar Source
- **Type**: Philosophical Commentary
- **Author**: ਸੰਤ ਸਿੰਘ ਮਸਕੀਨ (Sant Singh Maskeen)
- **Usage**:
  - Philosophical and ethical depth
  - Katha-based explanations
  - Contextual understanding
- **Caveat**: Not literal translation — vichaar (philosophical discourse) source only

### 12. English Interpretation – Dr. Manmohan Singh (1962)
- **Authority Level**: Reference Translation
- **Type**: SGPC-Published English Rendering
- **Author**: Dr. Manmohan Singh
- **Publisher**: SGPC
- **Usage**:
  - Widely used English meanings
  - Standard reference for English readers
- **Caveat**: Interpretive, not literal. Must always be labeled as "English interpretation based on Dr. Manmohan Singh"

---

## VI. MODERN ACADEMIC & INSTITUTIONAL SOURCES

*For structure, archaeology, and documentation.*

### 13. Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (SGPC)
- **Authority Level**: Institutional Authority
- **Type**: Official Sikh Institution
- **Key Documents**:
  - Sikh Rehat Maryada (1950)
  - Official Sikh institutional positions
  - Published works and translations
- **Status**: Not infallible, but authoritative within the Panth

### 14. Punjabi University Patiala
- **Authority Level**: Academic Authority
- **Type**: Academic Institution
- **Contributions**:
  - Sikh studies research
  - Manuscript preservation
  - Academic publications
  - Encyclopedia of Sikhism
- **Usage**: For scholarly references and research documentation

### 15. Sikh Reference Library (Pre & Post-1984)
- **Authority Level**: Archival Source
- **Type**: Manuscript Repository
- **Contents**:
  - Historical manuscripts
  - Hukamnamas (Royal edicts from Guru Sahiban)
  - Historical documents
- **Note**: Many documents lost in 1984; pre-1984 catalogues are critical references

---

## VII. CONTEMPORARY HISTORY & POLITICAL PERIOD (POST-1947)

*For modern figures, movements, and ongoing events.*

### Guidelines for Contemporary Sources

#### Mandatory Rules:
1. **Multiple Sources Required**: No single-source claims
2. **Separation of Facts and Interpretations**: Facts first, interpretations labeled
3. **No Opinion Journalism**: Editorial pieces are not sources
4. **Traceable Documentation Only**: Everything must be verifiable

#### Preferred Source Types:
- Court documents (official records)
- Public speeches (verbatim transcripts)
- Official statements (government, organizations)
- Chronological reporting (dated news from multiple outlets)
- Video/audio recordings (with clear provenance)

#### Editorial Standards:
- **No glorification** of any figure or movement
- **No vilification** of any figure or movement
- **Only traceable facts** with clear attribution
- **Contemporary disclaimer** always displayed

#### Special Handling Required:
- Events involving living persons
- Ongoing movements and developments
- Recent (past 5 years) occurrences
- Politically sensitive events

| Tier | Category | Examples | Usage |
|------|----------|----------|-------|
| **I** | Absolute Primary | Guru Granth Sahib Ji | Spiritual authority, interpretive lens |
| **II** | Classical Granths | Panth Prakash, Suraj Prakash, Bansavalinama | Primary historical narratives |
| **III** | Contemporary Accounts | Dabistan-i-Mazahib, Persian chronicles | Corroboration only |

## Citation Format

All content must include:

```
Source: [Title in Gurmukhi] / [Title in English]
Author: [If applicable]
Type: [Primary/Secondary/Teeka/Contemporary]
Year: [Composed/Published]
Location: [Chapter/Page/Verse if applicable]
Reliability Note: [Brief assessment]
Caveat: [Any known limitations or biases]
```

---

## Final Principles

1. **Gurbani First**: All history must be interpreted through the lens of Gurbani
2. **Source Transparency**: Every claim must answer "Says who?"
3. **Preserve Disagreements**: Conflicting interpretations are shown, not merged
4. **Label Everything**: Type, perspective, and caveats clearly marked
5. **No Hidden Biases**: Acknowledge perspectives openly
6. **Humility**: ਭੁੱਲ ਚੁੱਕ ਮਾਫ਼ ਕਰਨਾ (Please forgive any errors)
